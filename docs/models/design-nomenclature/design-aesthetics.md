# Design Aesthetics

Era-based and conceptual design movements that influence visual direction across all media.

---

## Conceptual Movements

### Minimalism

**Era:** 1960s art movement, ongoing in design

**Characteristics:**
- "Less is more" philosophy
- Maximum white space
- Limited color palette (often monochromatic)
- Essential elements only
- Clean typography, ample breathing room
- Removal of decorative elements

**When to use:**
- Luxury/premium brands
- Content-focused platforms
- When clarity is paramount
- Photography portfolios

**Examples:**
- [Apple](https://www.apple.com/)
- [Everlane](https://www.everlane.com/)
- [Aesop](https://www.aesop.com/)

**Key Question:** "Can I remove this element without losing meaning?"

---

### Maximalism

**Era:** Reaction to minimalism, 2010s resurgence

**Characteristics:**
- "More is more" philosophy
- Dense, layered compositions
- Bold colors, mixed patterns
- Multiple typefaces
- Visual abundance and complexity
- Eclectic mixing of elements

**When to use:**
- Creative/artistic brands
- Fashion and entertainment
- When personality > restraint
- Targeting younger demographics

**Examples:**
- [Gucci](https://www.gucci.com/)
- [Desigual](https://www.desigual.com/)
- [MTV](https://www.mtv.com/)

---

### Geometric

**Characteristics:**
- Based on circles, squares, triangles
- Mathematical precision
- Pattern and repetition
- Order and structure
- Often combined with minimalism

**When to use:**
- Tech and fintech
- Architecture firms
- Data visualization
- Modern corporate branding

**Examples:**
- [Stripe](https://stripe.com/)
- [Coinbase](https://www.coinbase.com/)

---

### Organic

**Characteristics:**
- Flowing, natural curves
- Irregular, hand-drawn quality
- Asymmetry
- Nature-inspired forms
- Soft, friendly feeling
- "Blob" shapes trending 2020s

**When to use:**
- Wellness and health brands
- Eco/sustainable products
- Approachable tech products
- Children's products

**Examples:**
- [Headspace](https://www.headspace.com/)
- [Notion](https://www.notion.so/) (illustrations)
- [Mailchimp](https://mailchimp.com/)

**CSS:** `border-radius` with irregular values, SVG blob shapes

## Era-Based Styles

### Art Nouveau

**Era:** 1890-1910

**Characteristics:**
- Organic, flowing lines ("whiplash curves")
- Natural motifs (flowers, vines, insects)
- Ornate, decorative
- Feminine figures
- Integration of form and structure

**When to use:**
- Vintage-inspired luxury
- Wine, perfume, boutique hotels
- Decorative accents

**Examples:**
- [Alfons Mucha artwork](https://www.muchafoundation.org/)
- Vintage Paris Metro entrances

---

### Art Deco

**Era:** 1920s-1930s

**Characteristics:**
- Geometric shapes (chevrons, sunbursts, zigzags)
- Symmetry and repetition
- Bold, contrasting colors
- Metallic accents (gold, silver)
- Luxury materials (marble, lacquer)
- "Machine age" elegance

**When to use:**
- Luxury/premium brands
- Hotels, real estate, finance
- Event invitations
- Craft cocktail bars

**Examples:**
- [The Great Gatsby movie design](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1343092/)
- Chrysler Building, Empire State Building
- [1920s-inspired Dribbble](https://dribbble.com/tags/art_deco)

**Color Palettes:** Gold + black, teal + gold, cream + burgundy

---

### Mid-Century Modern

**Era:** 1945-1970

**Characteristics:**
- Clean lines, organic curves
- Form follows function
- Natural materials (wood, leather)
- Atomic/space-age motifs
- Optimistic, forward-looking
- Muted earth tones + pops of color

**When to use:**
- Furniture and interior brands
- Architecture firms
- Sophisticated lifestyle brands
- Retro-modern aesthetics

**Examples:**
- [Herman Miller](https://www.hermanmiller.com/)
- [Design Within Reach](https://www.dwr.com/)
- Mad Men visual style

---

### Retro / Vintage

**Era:** Various (typically 1950s-1990s references)

**Characteristics:**
- Nostalgic color palettes
- Period-appropriate typography
- Grainy textures, worn effects
- Analog/handmade feel
- Specific era signifiers (50s diner, 70s disco, 80s neon)

**Subcategories:**

| Era | Visual Markers |
|-----|----------------|
| **1950s** | Pastels, atomic shapes, script fonts |
| **1960s** | Psychedelic, op-art, bold shapes |
| **1970s** | Earth tones, rounded sans, disco gold |
| **1980s** | Neon, grid lines, chrome, synthwave |
| **1990s** | Grunge, bright primaries, geometric |

**Examples:**
- [Stranger Things branding](https://www.netflix.com/title/80057281)
- [Synthwave aesthetic](https://dribbble.com/tags/synthwave)

---

### Y2K

**Era:** Late 1990s - early 2000s (resurgence 2020s)

**Characteristics:**
- Chrome and metallic textures
- 3D rendered elements
- Bubbly, inflated shapes
- Gradient meshes
- Futuristic optimism
- Tech-utopian aesthetic
- Low-res pixelation (intentional)

**When to use:**
- Gen Z targeting
- Music and entertainment
- Fashion forward brands
- Nostalgic tech products

**Examples:**
- [Y2K Aesthetic Institute](https://www.instagram.com/y2k.institute/)
- [Olivia Rodrigo album art](https://oliviarodrigo.com/)

---

### Futurism / Sci-Fi

**Characteristics:**
- Sleek, streamlined forms
- Dark backgrounds with glowing elements
- Monospace/tech typography
- Motion blur, speed lines
- Holographic effects
- Minimal color with neon accents

**When to use:**
- Tech startups
- Gaming
- Automotive
- AI/ML products

**Examples:**
- [Cyberpunk 2077](https://www.cyberpunk.net/)
- [SpaceX](https://www.spacex.com/)
- [Tesla](https://www.tesla.com/)

---

### Retro-Futurism

**Era:** Emerged 1960s-1980s, revived 2010s

**Characteristics:**
- How the past imagined the future
- Combines nostalgia with innovation
- Space age meets analog
- Chrome, fins, atomic motifs
- Optimistic technological vision

**When to use:**
- Innovative products with heritage
- Automotive (electric vehicles)
- Smart home products
- Hospitality with character

**Examples:**
- [The Jetsons aesthetic](https://www.warnerbros.com/)
- [Fallout game series](https://fallout.bethesda.net/)

---

### Psychedelic

**Era:** 1960s, periodic revivals

**Characteristics:**
- Intense, saturated colors
- Warped, distorted forms
- Complex patterns
- Optical illusions
- Hand-drawn lettering
- Counter-culture associations

**When to use:**
- Music and festivals
- Cannabis industry
- Artistic expression
- Alternative culture

**Examples:**
- [Grateful Dead artwork](https://www.dead.net/)
- [Tame Impala visuals](https://tameimpala.com/)

---

### Grunge

**Era:** Late 1980s - early 1990s

**Characteristics:**
- Distressed, worn textures
- Rough, raw edges
- Dark, muted colors
- Layered, collage-like compositions
- Anti-establishment, DIY aesthetic
- Photocopied quality

**When to use:**
- Music and entertainment
- Counter-culture brands
- Authentic, unpolished feel
- Youth marketing

**Examples:**
- [Nirvana album art](https://nirvana.com/)
- [Thrasher Magazine](https://www.thrashermagazine.com/)

---

### Punk

**Era:** 1970s onward

**Characteristics:**
- DIY construction (cut and paste)
- Ransom note typography
- High contrast (often B&W)
- Intentionally crude
- Anti-design as design
- Provocative imagery

**When to use:**
- Music industry
- Rebellious brands
- Street fashion
- Political messaging

**Examples:**
- Sex Pistols artwork
- [Vivienne Westwood](https://www.viviennewestwood.com/)

---

### Swiss / International Style

**Era:** 1950s-1970s Switzerland

**Characteristics:**
- Grid-based layouts
- Sans-serif typography (Helvetica, Univers)
- Objective, factual presentation
- Asymmetric layouts within grid
- Photography over illustration
- Left-aligned text

**When to use:**
- Corporate communications
- Wayfinding and signage
- When neutrality is valued
- Information-heavy layouts

**Examples:**
- Swiss tourism posters
- [Massimo Vignelli's work](https://vfrm.co/)
- NYC subway signage
