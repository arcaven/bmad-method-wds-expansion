# Typography Classification

The systematic categorization of typefaces, enabling precise communication about font choices and their appropriate applications.

---

## Primary Categories

### Serif

**Definition:** Typefaces with small decorative strokes (serifs) at the ends of letters.

**Characteristics:**
- Traditional, established feel
- High readability in print
- Conveys authority, trust, heritage
- Subcategories: Old Style, Transitional, Didone, Slab

**When to use:**
- Long-form body text (print)
- Legal, financial, educational contexts
- Luxury and heritage brands
- Headlines seeking gravitas

**Examples:**
- Times New Roman, Georgia (Transitional)
- Garamond, Palatino (Old Style)
- Bodoni, Didot (Didone/Modern)
- Playfair Display (Display Serif)

**Popular Web Serifs:**
- [Source Serif Pro](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Source+Serif+Pro)
- [Merriweather](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Merriweather)
- [Lora](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Lora)

---

### Sans-Serif

**Definition:** Typefaces without serifs ("sans" = without).

**Characteristics:**
- Modern, clean appearance
- Excellent screen readability
- Versatile across contexts
- Subcategories: Grotesque, Neo-Grotesque, Geometric, Humanist

**When to use:**
- Digital interfaces (UI/UX)
- Modern brands
- Body text on screens
- Headlines and short text

---

## Sans-Serif Subcategories

### Grotesque

**Era:** 19th century, earliest sans-serifs

**Characteristics:**
- Quirky, irregular proportions
- Visible stroke contrast in some letters
- "G" often has a spur
- Squarish curves
- Characterful, less uniform

**Examples:**
- Akzidenz Grotesk
- Franklin Gothic
- News Gothic

**When to use:** Headlines, branding seeking vintage modern feel

---

### Neo-Grotesque

**Era:** Mid-20th century (Swiss Style)

**Characteristics:**
- Refined grotesque with more uniformity
- Minimal stroke contrast
- Neutral, anonymous character
- Highly legible at all sizes
- The "workhorse" of typography

**Examples:**
- Helvetica
- Arial
- Univers
- [Inter](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Inter)

**When to use:** UI text, corporate communications, anywhere neutrality is valued

---

### Geometric

**Era:** 1920s-1930s (Bauhaus influence)

**Characteristics:**
- Based on geometric shapes (circles, squares, triangles)
- Uniform stroke weight
- Single-story "a" and "g" common
- Modern, futuristic feel
- Can sacrifice readability for form

**Examples:**
- Futura
- Avant Garde
- Century Gothic
- [Poppins](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Poppins)
- [Outfit](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Outfit)

**When to use:** Headlines, logos, modern/tech brands

**Readability Note:** Less ideal for long body text due to uniform shapes.

---

### Humanist

**Era:** 1930s onward

**Characteristics:**
- Calligraphic influence (pen-drawn quality)
- Variable stroke width
- Open counters (letter openings)
- Warmer, more approachable than geometric
- Double-story "a" and "g" common
- Excellent readability

**Examples:**
- Gill Sans
- Frutiger
- Verdana
- [Open Sans](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Open+Sans)
- [Lato](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Lato)
- [Source Sans Pro](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Source+Sans+3)

**When to use:** Body text, interfaces requiring warmth, healthcare, education

## Specialty Categories

### Slab Serif (Egyptian)

**Characteristics:**
- Heavy, block-like serifs
- Minimal or no bracketing (serif connection curve)
- Bold, sturdy appearance
- High impact for headlines
- Subtypes: Geometric slab, Humanist slab

**Examples:**
- Rockwell
- Courier (monospace slab)
- [Roboto Slab](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Roboto+Slab)
- [Zilla Slab](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Zilla+Slab)

**When to use:** Headlines, posters, strong statements, tech documentation

---

### Monospace

**Characteristics:**
- Every character occupies same width
- Originally for typewriters and code
- Technical, systematic feel
- Easy character alignment

**Examples:**
- Courier New
- [Fira Code](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Fira+Code) (with ligatures)
- [JetBrains Mono](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/JetBrains+Mono)
- [IBM Plex Mono](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/IBM+Plex+Mono)

**When to use:** Code blocks, technical content, data displays, brutalist design

---

### Display / Decorative

**Characteristics:**
- Designed for large sizes (headlines, logos)
- High personality, unique features
- Poor readability at small sizes
- Often custom or limited character sets

**Examples:**
- Impact
- Bebas Neue
- [Abril Fatface](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Abril+Fatface)
- [Playfair Display](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Playfair+Display)

**When to use:** Headlines, logos, posters — NEVER body text

---

### Script / Handwriting

**Characteristics:**
- Mimics handwritten or calligraphic text
- Connecting or non-connecting letterforms
- Ranges from formal (Copperplate) to casual (Comic Sans)
- Use sparingly

**Examples:**
- Brush Script
- [Dancing Script](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Dancing+Script)
- [Caveat](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Caveat)

**When to use:** Invitations, signatures, accents, personal branding

**Warning:** Overuse appears unprofessional. Never for body text.

---

### Blackletter (Gothic)

**Characteristics:**
- Medieval manuscript style
- Angular, complex letterforms
- Low legibility for modern readers
- Historical, traditional, or edgy connotations

**Examples:**
- Fraktur
- Old English
- Textura

**When to use:** Logos (newspapers, metal bands), certificates, Halloween

## Typography Anatomy Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Baseline** | Line where letters sit |
| **X-height** | Height of lowercase "x" (affects readability) |
| **Cap height** | Height of capital letters |
| **Ascender** | Part of letter extending above x-height (b, d, h) |
| **Descender** | Part extending below baseline (g, p, y) |
| **Counter** | Enclosed or partially enclosed space in letter |
| **Stem** | Main vertical stroke |
| **Bowl** | Curved stroke enclosing counter |
| **Serif** | Small decorative stroke at ends |
| **Terminal** | End of stroke without serif |

## Spacing Terms

| Term | Definition | CSS Property |
|------|------------|--------------|
| **Kerning** | Space between specific letter pairs | — (font-level) |
| **Tracking** | Overall letter spacing | `letter-spacing` |
| **Leading** | Vertical space between lines | `line-height` |
| **Word spacing** | Space between words | `word-spacing` |

## Font Pairing Principles

1. **Contrast** — Pair serif with sans-serif
2. **Similarity** — Match x-heights and proportions
3. **Hierarchy** — Display for headlines, text for body
4. **Limit** — Maximum 2-3 typefaces per project

**Proven Pairings:**
- Playfair Display + Source Sans Pro
- Montserrat + Merriweather
- Poppins + Lora
- Inter + Fraunces
